STATEMENT OF NEED

The Journey from Battlefield to Streets

On battlefields across Iraq, Afghanistan, and conflicts spanning generations, service members have endured unimaginable trauma. They've witnessed the chaos of combat, carried wounded brothers and sisters to safety, and made split-second decisions under fire that haunt them still. Many returned home with visible wounds—amputated limbs, traumatic brain injuries, burns, and shrapnel scars. Others carry invisible wounds—post-traumatic stress disorder, moral injury, depression, and the psychological toll of warfare that society cannot see but that weighs just as heavily.

These warriors, who once relied on unit cohesion and military structure, often find themselves struggling to navigate civilian life. The transition from battlefield to home front can be more treacherous than combat itself. Without the framework of military service, without the camaraderie of their units, many veterans find themselves isolated and adrift. Mental health challenges go untreated. Substance use becomes a means of self-medication. Jobs are lost. Relationships deteriorate. Housing becomes unstable.

The same veterans who answered their nation's call and stood ready to sacrifice everything now find themselves sleeping under bridges, in abandoned buildings, or on the streets of the communities they once protected. They face this battle alone, often too proud to ask for help, sometimes unaware that help exists, and frequently unable to navigate the complex systems designed to serve them.

Our program recognizes that these veterans—wounded in body, mind, or spirit—deserve more than bureaucratic processes and institutional barriers. They deserve face-to-face human connection, immediate assistance without red tape, and a pathway back to stability that honors their service and restores their dignity.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Program Model: Community Thrift Store as Service Hub

Our innovative approach centers on a community thrift store that serves three critical functions:

1. Donation Collection Center

  • Accept clothing, household items, hygiene products, and essential goods from community donors
  • Build community awareness and engagement around veteran homelessness
  • Create sustainable resource pipeline independent of grant funding

2. Direct Service Delivery Platform

  • Provide free essential items to homeless veterans through face-to-face distribution
  • Create dignified access to necessities without barriers or bureaucracy
  • Establish regular touchpoints for relationship building and needs assessment

3. Data Collection and Case Management Hub

  • Document each veteran interaction and items distributed
  • Conduct intake assessments during item distribution
  • Track service utilization patterns and individual progress over time
  • Identify urgent needs and make appropriate referrals

Thrift Store Operations

  • Staffing: [Number] paid staff + [Number] volunteers

Face-to-Face Outreach Program

Data Collection Protocol

During each interaction, we will collect:

  • Basic demographic information (age, service era, disability status)
  • Current housing status and duration of homelessness
  • Immediate needs (clothing, hygiene items, medical, mental health)
  • Service utilization history
  • Barriers to housing and employment
  • Contact information for follow-up (when possible)

Partnership and Referral Network

  • VA Medical Center and Veterans Services
  • Local housing authorities and shelters
  • Mental health and substance abuse treatment providers
  • Employment and job training programs
  • Legal aid services
